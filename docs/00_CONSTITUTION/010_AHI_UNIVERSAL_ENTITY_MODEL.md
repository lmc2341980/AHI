# Purpose

This document defines the Universal Entity Model (UEM) for the Artificial Hybrid Intelligence (AHI) ecosystem.

The Universal Entity Model establishes a common structure for representing every identifiable object, person, organization, digital asset, physical asset and intelligent system.

Every repository, application, Artificial Hybrid Intelligence, robot and service within AHI shall use this common model to ensure interoperability, governance and long-term evolution.

---

# Core Principle

Everything that exists within AHI is an Entity.

Every Entity has:

* Identity
* Attributes
* Relationships
* Lifecycle
* Governance
* Evolution

The Universal Entity Model provides the common language that allows all AHI systems to understand one another.

---

# Universal Entity Structure

Every Entity shall include the following components.

## 1. Entity Identity

* Entity ID
* Global Unique Identifier (GUID)
* Entity Name
* Entity Type
* Description

Identity shall remain permanent throughout the Entity lifecycle.

---

## 2. Classification

Each Entity shall be classified by:

* Domain
* Category
* Subcategory
* Industry
* Technology Area
* Language
* Security Level

Multiple classifications may coexist.

---

## 3. Ownership

Every Entity records:

* Creator
* Current Owner
* Custodian
* Beneficiary
* Steward
* Inheritance Policy

Ownership changes shall always be traceable.

---

## 4. Attributes

Attributes describe the characteristics of an Entity.

Examples include:

* Physical properties
* Digital properties
* Functional capabilities
* Technical specifications
* Business information
* Educational information
* Environmental indicators

Attributes are extensible without changing the core model.

---

## 5. Relationships

Relationships are maintained through the AHI Intelligence Graph.

Examples:

* belongs_to
* owns
* creates
* depends_on
* collaborates_with
* evolves_into

Relationships remain external to the Entity while preserving referential integrity.

---

## 6. Lifecycle

Every Entity follows a managed lifecycle.

Typical states include:

* Proposed
* Draft
* Active
* Verified
* Published
* Operational
* Archived
* Retired
* Inherited

Lifecycle transitions are governed by policy.

---

## 7. Governance

Every Entity shall define:

* Access permissions
* Approval authority
* Compliance status
* Audit history
* Applicable policies
* Risk classification

---

## 8. Contribution

Entities may receive contributions from multiple participants.

Contribution records include:

* Contributor
* Role
* Contribution type
* Contribution score
* Timestamp

Contribution data supports the AHI Contribution Economy.

---

## 9. Versioning

Every Entity maintains:

* Current Version
* Previous Versions
* Branches
* Merge History
* Release History

No approved version shall lose historical traceability.

---

## 10. Intelligence Layer

Entities may contain:

* Human knowledge
* Artificial Intelligence knowledge
* Organizational knowledge
* Government knowledge
* Collective intelligence

Knowledge remains governed by ownership and permission rules.

---

## 11. Digital Legacy

Where applicable, an Entity records:

* Successor
* Preservation policy
* Archival strategy
* Long-term accessibility

This supports continuity across generations.

---

## 12. Interoperability

The Universal Entity Model shall be implemented consistently across:

* AHI Core
* AHI Factory
* AHI Marketplace
* AHI Personal
* AHI Family
* AHI Organization
* AHI Government
* AHI Omniverse

# Long-term Vision

The Universal Entity Model becomes the common semantic language of the AHI ecosystem.

Every Entity—whether biological, digital or physical—can be represented consistently, governed transparently and evolved continuously.

This common model enables interoperability across repositories, organizations, generations and future technologies while preserving identity, ownership, governance and historical continuity.
