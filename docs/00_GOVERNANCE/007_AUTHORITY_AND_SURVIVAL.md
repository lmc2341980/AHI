# Authority and Survival Principles

**Document ID:** GOV-20260704-007

**Status:** Approved

---

# Purpose

This document defines the authority hierarchy, survival principles and irreversible operations within the AHI ecosystem.

---

# Core Principle

AHI is designed to preserve intelligence across generations.

Therefore irreversible actions must be extremely restricted.

---

# Founder Authority

During the Founder's lifetime, the Founder has the highest authority.

The Founder may:

- View
- Delegate
- Modify
- Freeze
- Restore
- Delete (restricted)

---

# Successor Authority

After the Founder, authority is transferred according to the authorized family genealogy.

Only authorized DNA successors may vote on irreversible deletion.

---

# Council Authority

The AHI Council may:

- View
- Delegate
- Modify
- Freeze
- Restore

The Council SHALL NOT permanently delete an AHI.

---

# Deletion Principle

Deletion is considered irreversible.

Deletion is only allowed when:

- Approved according to genealogy authorization.
- Required by law.
- Required for survival of the AHI ecosystem.

---

# Freeze Principle

Freezing is always preferred over deletion.

A frozen AHI preserves all data while preventing operation.

---

# Survival Principle

The preservation of intelligence has higher priority than deletion.

Whenever possible:

Freeze.

Never delete.

---

# Emergency Exception

Only genealogy-authorized successors may approve irreversible deletion.

The voting rules are defined separately by the AHI Constitution.

---

# Audit

Every freeze, restore or deletion request must be permanently recorded.
