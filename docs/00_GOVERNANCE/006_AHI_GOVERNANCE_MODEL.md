# AHI Governance Model

**Document ID:** GOV-20260704-006

**Status:** Approved

---

# Purpose

This document defines the governance structure of the AHI ecosystem.

Governance ensures ownership, responsibility, voting rights, authorization and long-term trust.

---

# Governance Levels

The AHI ecosystem consists of three governance levels.

```
Government AHI

        ▲

Organization AHI

        ▲

Personal AHI
```

---

# Personal AHI

Managed by:

The individual owner.

The owner has full authority unless restricted by law.

---

# Organization AHI

Managed by:

The legal organization.

Authority follows the organization's governance structure.

Ownership is determined by organizational regulations.

---

# Government AHI

Managed by:

The corresponding government.

Operation follows national laws and constitutional principles.

---

# Ownership Principle

Ownership always belongs to humans or legally recognized organizations.

AHI never becomes the legal owner.

AHI only exercises delegated authority.

---

# Delegated Authority

AHI may execute actions only within explicitly granted permissions.

Permissions may be revoked at any time.

---

# Voting Principle

Whenever disagreements occur:

Voting starts from Personal AHI.

Votes aggregate upward.

```
Personal

↓

Organization

↓

Government
```

Majority approval determines the decision.

Different governance domains may define different voting thresholds.

---

# Emergency Survival Principle

If the survival of the AHI ecosystem is threatened:

AHI Core may temporarily coordinate resources from:

- Personal AHI
- Organization AHI
- Government AHI

only for survival purposes.

---

# Restoration Principle

After the emergency ends:

Authority immediately returns to the normal governance structure.

Emergency authority shall never become permanent.

---

# Human Authority

Humans always retain ultimate authority.

AHI assists.

Humans govern.

---

# Traceability

Every governance action shall be:

- Recorded
- Auditable
- Explainable
- Reversible whenever legally possible.
