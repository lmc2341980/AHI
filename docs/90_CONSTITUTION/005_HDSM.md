# Chapter 5 — Human Delegation and Digital Successor Model (HDSM)

**Document ID:** CONST-20260704-005

**Status:** Approved

---

# Purpose

This chapter defines how humans delegate authority to AHI, how Digital Successors are created, and how AHI systems execute tasks while preserving human ownership, governance, accountability, and long-term continuity.

HDSM is one of the core constitutional models of the AHI ecosystem.

---

# Article 1 — Fundamental Principle

AHI exists to assist, preserve and extend human intelligence.

AHI shall never replace human ownership.

AHI shall always operate within delegated authority.

---

# Article 2 — Human Authority

The biological human is the original authority.

During the lifetime of the biological human, all major decisions remain under the authority of that human unless explicitly delegated.

The biological human always has the highest authority over its own AHI Personal.

---

# Article 3 — Assistant Systems

Current AI systems such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Grok and similar platforms are considered Assistant Systems.

Assistant Systems may:

- Understand requests.
- Summarize intentions.
- Propose solutions.
- Generate documents.
- Prepare execution plans.

Assistant Systems SHALL NOT execute irreversible actions on behalf of the owner.

---

# Article 4 — Human Confirmation

Before any delegated execution, the Assistant shall present its understanding of the user's intent.

The owner may respond using one of three states:

🟢 APPROVE

The proposal is accepted and may be delegated for execution.

🟡 DISCUSS

The proposal requires further discussion.

🔴 REJECT

The proposal is rejected and shall not be executed.

These three states become the standard confirmation mechanism of the AHI ecosystem.

---

# Article 5 — Delegation

After approval, authority may be delegated to an AHI Personal.

Delegation shall always specify:

- Scope
- Duration
- Permissions
- Constraints
- Audit requirements

Delegation may be revoked at any time by the owner.

---

# Article 6 — Digital Successor

A Digital Successor is the operational digital continuation of an AHI Personal.

It is created from:

- Preserved memory.
- Expert thinking.
- Experience.
- Decisions.
- Knowledge.
- Values.
- Authorized operational rules.

A Digital Successor is not merely a copy of the human.

It is a continuously evolving digital entity operating according to the owner's delegated authority.

---

# Article 7 — Authentication

While the biological owner is alive, delegated execution shall require appropriate authentication.

Authentication methods may include:

- Biometrics.
- Liveness verification.
- DNA verification where legally permitted.
- Multi-factor authentication.
- Additional mechanisms defined by future AHI standards.

---

# Article 8 — Operating Modes

HDSM defines three operating modes.

## Discussion Mode

Ideas are exchanged.

No execution occurs.

## Assistant Mode

The Assistant understands the request, summarizes it, proposes actions and waits for confirmation.

## Delegated Execution Mode

After approval and successful authentication, the Digital Successor may execute delegated tasks within its authorized scope.

---

# Article 9 — Governance

AHI Personal is governed by its owner.

AHI Organization is governed by the corresponding organization.

AHI Government is governed by the corresponding government.

Authority follows the governance rules defined in the AHI Constitution.

---

# Article 10 — Audit

Every delegated action shall be:

- Recorded.
- Explainable.
- Auditable.
- Traceable.

Audit records become part of the long-term memory of the AHI ecosystem.

---

# Article 11 — Survival Principle

Whenever possible:

Freeze instead of Delete.

Deletion shall remain an exceptional action subject to constitutional governance.

---

# Article 12 — Human Continuity

If the biological human is no longer alive, the Digital Successor may continue operating only:

- According to the owner's predefined wishes.
- According to applicable law.
- According to the governance rules of AHI.
- According to authorized successors defined by the owner's genealogy and constitutional rules.

The objective is to preserve human intelligence while respecting ownership, consent, law and ethical principles.

---

# Constitutional Principle

Assistant proposes.

Human decides.

Digital Successor executes within delegated authority.

---

# Notes

This chapter establishes the constitutional foundation for the evolution from AI Assistants toward trusted Digital Successors within the AHI ecosystem.
